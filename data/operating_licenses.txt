All traders and service providers must obtain licenses under the Shop Licence Act [Chapter 14:17].

## Business Licensing

- Applications are submitted through Health Inspectors at local clinics.
- Inspections cover health, fire safety, and structural compliance.
- Fees vary by business type and premises size.

## Lodge Licensing Conditions

- Lodges must not host weddings or noisy events.
- Liquor sales are prohibited.
- Directional signage must be approved.
- Facilities must be disability-friendly.
- Onsite parking is mandatory.
- Council may revoke licenses for violations.

## Required Documents

- Building plans (if applicable)
- Health clearance
- Fire safety certificate
- Proof of premises ownership or lease

## Application Procedure

Applicants must complete Form S.L.2 under the Shop Licences Act, 1976. The form includes:

- Type of application:
  - New licence
  - Extension or removal of trade
  - Alteration of licence conditions

- Applicant details:
  - Full name, residential and business address
  - Date and place of birth
  - Ownership status of the business
  - For companies: details of all directors or committee members

- Control of business:
  - Name and address of person in actual control of the premises

- Trade details:
  - Name under which business will operate
  - Goods to be sold (classified or specified)
  - Method of sale: auction, vending machine, out-of-hand
  - Trading type: wholesaler, retailer, or both

- Premises details:
  - Urban: stand number, street, building name, shop number
  - Rural: plot number, farm name, mining location (if applicable)

- Building status:
  - If premises are under construction or require alterations, attach approved plans and completion timeline

- Legal compliance:
  - Attach any required authority under other laws or by-laws
  - Submit proof of planning consent and newspaper advertisement of intent

## Classes of Goods

Licences are issued based on the class of goods to be traded:

- Class 1: Fishmonger goods
- Class 2: Butcher and fishmonger goods
- Class 3: Dairy produce and eggs
- Class 4: Frozen, smoked, salted, or dried meats and fish
- Class 5: Foodstuffs for off-premises consumption
- Class 6: All other foodstuffs
- Class 7: Non-food goods
- Class 8: Packaged foods, beverages, tobacco, newspapers, fruit, vegetables
- Class 9: Aerated drinks, tobacco products, sweets, bread, ice cream

## Legal Notes

- Submit applications in duplicate.
- False statements may result in cancellation and legal penalties.
- A licence does not exempt the holder from other laws governing the business.
- Convictions under the Shop Licences Act, Shop Hours Act, or Trade Measures Act must be disclosed.
